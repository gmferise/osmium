Osmium Privacy Policy

It is understandable to be concerned when an application is asking access to manage your files and Google account.
This document is written to be easily read and understood by any user and to comply with the Google API Services User Data Policy.

Google Permissions and Scopes

When making requests to Google's API, we ask for permission to different types of requests in the form of scopes.
A scope is essentially a bundle of permissions. We use as few scopes as possible to make our app work.
Here are the scopes we request access to and why we need them.

Scope Permissions Granted Explanation
/auth/spreadsheets See, edit, create, and delete your spreadsheets in Google Drive Used to create new databases and edit the contents of existing databases
/auth/drive.file View and manage Google Drive files and folders that you have opened or created with this app Used to view and manage databases created with our app
/auth/drive.metadata.readonly View metadata for files in your Google Drive Used to search for databases in your Google Drive

Additional Google Data

It should also be noted that we use very basic profile data from your Google account to personalize
the appearance of our app when you are signed in. This includes your account email, name, and profile photo.
None of this data is ever stored and is simply pulled from your account each time you authenticate.

Data Collection and Storage

This app is a user-friendly interface for a Google spreadsheet which provides convenient data storage.
All user-driven data collection is sourced from and entered to the selected Google spreadsheet.
The app does not collect or store any data excluding user-created data within each Google spreadsheet. Its web interface is hosted solely on GitHub Pages, which does not support server-side data storage.
It does not communicate with any external websites or servers excluding Google's Services and APIs.
